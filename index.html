Nyigisha: The Digital Bridge to Rwanda's Vision 2050

"Nyigisha" means "teach me", and that is exactly what millions of learners are asking for today.

The Problem We're Solving

The crisis is three-fold:

  • The Classroom Squeeze: While we've built 11,000 classrooms, P1 ratios remain as high as 76:1
  • The Shadow Education Trap: 40% of parents are paying for 'mentors' who lack training
  • The Comprehension Ceiling: World Bank data shows only 37.5% of P3 students are proficient in English

Most digital learning platforms available today are English-only, especially in science subjects. For students who have not fully mastered academic English, comprehension becomes a barrier instead of a bridge. The problem is not a lack of effort ‚Äî it is inefficiency, inequality, and inaccessibility.

Our Team

We aren't just students; we are a fusion of youth innovators, educators, and tech-driven problem solvers. We have lived the challenges of the Rwandan classroom, and we have the combined technical and entrepreneurial mindset to fix it.

Entrepreneurship

Kwiga ubucuruzi mu rurimi rwawe - Learn Business in Your Language

Video Lesson: Multiple Choice Questions

üìö Lesson Overview

This lesson covers entrepreneurship fundamentals with multiple choice questions explained in Kinyarwanda by Teacher Cedric. Perfect for students preparing for exams or wanting to understand business concepts in their native language.

üéØ Key Topics Covered

  • Ibisobanuro by'ubucuruzi (Business Definitions)
  • Ubwoko bw'ubucuruzi (Types of Business)
  • Inyungu n'ingaruka z'ubucuruzi (Benefits and Challenges of Business)
  • Amasoko n'abaguzi (Markets and Customers)
  • Gukora isesengura ry'isoko (Market Analysis)

üí° Why Learn in Kinyarwanda?

Research shows that students understand concepts better when taught in their mother tongue. At Nyigisha, we believe that language should never be a barrier to learning. Our bilingual approach ensures:

  • Better comprehension of complex business concepts
  • Increased confidence in exam preparation
  • Connection between classroom theory and real-world application
  • Accessibility for rural students with limited English proficiency
